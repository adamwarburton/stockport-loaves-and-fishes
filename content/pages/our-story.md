## It started with a car boot

Over a decade ago, our founder Ed Leavy was told there were "no homeless in Stockport". He went out one evening to see for himself.

What he found was people — cold, hungry, and walked past. So he started turning up with hot food in the boot of his car.

A handful of people came at first. Then more. These days, dozens of guests sit down with us for dinner every Sunday evening at Stockport Baptist Church, and more again at Christmas.

## Who we are now

We're a small team of volunteers — cooks, drivers, washers-up and champion talkers. Nobody is paid. We're a registered charity (number 1200660) and the whole operation runs on roughly £15,000 a year: every Sunday dinner, every coat, every sleeping bag, every food parcel.

Dinner is the heart of it, but it's not all of it. We hand out warm clothes, bedding and tents, make up food parcels, and help our guests take the next step — whatever that looks like for them, including for refugees and people waiting on asylum decisions.

Mostly, though, we sit down and eat together. Dinner's better with company.

## The faith bit

We're a Christian charity. The clue's in the name — loaves, fishes, and everybody gets fed.

Our faith is why we cook. It's not the price of admission. You'll never be asked what you believe, and there's no sermon between you and your dinner. Everyone's welcome at the table — that's the whole point.
