## What happens

You walk in. You sit down. Someone brings you a hot dinner and a brew.

Nobody will ask why you've come. Nobody will ask you to fill anything in. If you want to talk, we're good at that. If you'd rather eat in peace, that's fine too.

If you're sleeping rough, sofa-surfing, or just can't stretch the money to the end of the week — this is for you.

## What we can give you

- A hot dinner and something warm to drink
- Coats, hats, gloves and warm clothes
- Sleeping bags and tents
- A food parcel to take with you
- A friendly face and time to talk
- Help working out what comes next — including if you're a refugee or waiting on an asylum decision

If we haven't got the thing you need this week, tell us. We'll do our best to have it next Sunday.

## What we can't do

We'll be straight with you, because it matters:

- We're only open on Sunday evenings.
- We don't have beds or rooms.
- We can't sort housing on the spot — but the people below can help, and we'll happily sit with you while you ring them.

## Finding us

We're at **Stockport Baptist Church, Thomson Street, Stockport SK3 9DR** — a short walk from the town centre. Come round to the open door; if you're not sure, ask anyone in an apron.
